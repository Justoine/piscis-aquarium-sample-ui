BottleNose Dolphin

Bottlenose dolphins can be found all around the world in warm water. They can be found both close to shore in shallow water and far out in deep, dark water. Dolphins have a lot of trouble getting caught in the trash that people dump on the beach.So we have to make sure we don't litter in the ocean.

Sea Turtle

A long, streamlined shell distinguishes sea turtles. Sea turtles can be olive-green, yellow, greenish-brown, reddish-brown, or black, depending on the species. Swimming limbs and flippers have been developed. Male and female sea turtles are the same size as adults. Sea turtles reproduct from 1-3 years in the beach. Usually when hatching season came, Piscis Aquarium shows them in The Hatch show on every March!

Manta-Ray

Manta Rays can be found in tropical, subtropical, and temperate oceans all around the world. In Spanish, the word "manta" means "blanket" or "cloak," referring to the animals' huge, flat, diamond-shaped body with triangular pectoral fins. Manta rays are known as "devil fish" because of the two horn-shaped fins that protrude from the front of their heads.

Hammerhead Shark

Great hammerhead sharks are apex predators that can be found in coastal, warm waters with a temperature of 68°F (20°C) or higher all around the world. Great hammerhead sharks, unlike scalloped hammerhead sharks, are solitary and migrate alone over distances of up to 756 miles (1,200 km). Great hammerhead sharks also grow at a faster rate than other hammerhead shark species, reaching maturity between the ages of 5 and 9.

Dugong

Dugongs are huge, robust creatures with paddle-like front flippers and a propeller-like tail with a straight or concave edge. Their tail distinguishes them from manatees, which have a paddle-shaped tail. Dugongs have fins that mimic those of dolphins, however they don't have a dorsal fin like dolphins.Dugong's can live up to 70 years or more! Older than an average human!

Basking Shark

The basking shark is the world's second largest fish, behind the whale shark, which is also a filter feeder. It possesses a conical snout and gill slits that run virtually the whole circumference of its head. Gill rakers are structures that are associated with the gills. The basking shark can grow up to 10 meters long, but the normal size is 7-9 meters, and it can live for up to 50 years. It is a migratory species with a large range.
